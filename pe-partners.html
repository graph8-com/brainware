Partnering with Private Equity —

A Smarter Path to High-Value Acquisitions

At Brainware, we build, scale, and transition high-growth, profitable businesses, making them ideal acquisition targets for Private Equity (PE) firms. Unlike traditional venture-backed startups that burn cash chasing growth, our companies are structured for profitability, clean equity structures, and seamless majority ownership transitions.

Our Model Benefits

Cash-flow Positive

Profitable and self-sustaining, not reliant on venture funding.

Founder-Led

Leadership team retains significant equity and long-term commitment.

Built for Scale

Reaching $50M+ in revenue before transitioning majority ownership.

How Our PE Partnership Model Works

1

We Build the Business

We identify, fund, and launch businesses, setting up operations and securing first customers to validate product-market fit and early profitability.

2

Scaling to $50M+ in Revenue

Our companies are structured to reach $50M+ ARR profitably, using sustainable growth strategies without excessive outside capital.

3

PE Acquisition (50% Majority Ownership)

At the optimal growth stage ($50M+ revenue), Brainware sells 45% of its equity (maintaining a 10% stake), while founders sell 5% or more to a PE firm, giving them immediate majority control while ensuring strong leadership continuity.

4

Long-Term Growth & Exit Strategy

Post-acquisition, the business scales further under PE ownership, with the highly motivated founding team still owning 45% of the company—ensuring continued success.

Why Brainware Businesses Are Perfect for PE Firms

Immediate Majority Control

PE firms can acquire 50% outright, gaining majority ownership without dilution risks or investor negotiations.

Clean Cap Tables

No multiple funding rounds. No preferred shares. Just a simple, founder-friendly ownership structure.

Proven Profitability

Companies scale organically with strong cash flows and EBITDA-positive operations.

Retained Leadership

Co-founders retain 45% equity and have long-term upside—keeping them fully committed post-acquisition.
